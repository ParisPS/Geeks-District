Star Wars

In May 1977, Star Wars arrived in theaters in the USA, the first film in a billion dollar franchise in theaters that earned its creator, George Lucas, enormous prestige and an empire in licensed products. From the beginning, more than a creative vision of a concise universe and populated by strange and fascinating beings, the filmmaker valued a different approach to commercializing the film - managing to retain the rights to merchandising his work, which made him one of the most powerful men in the entertainment industry - decreeing the death of Old Hollywood and its current business format.
